Third Code

configuration:

  • Also known as PVC (polyvinyl chloride) or polyvinyl chloride.

  • Strong, flexible and soft due to chemical softening.

How to use:

    • It can be found in:
    • shower curtains, cleaning bottles, tubes, cooking oil bottles, window and door frames.
    • floors, and clear food wraps.
    • sheathing material for computer cables, plumbing parts, and in garden hoses.

  • Do not use it in cooking.

  • Try not to store food in it.

  • Check the material of children's toys and inflatable objects to ensure that they do not contain it.

How to get rid:


  • If you need to dispose of either item, ask your local waste department to see if you should put it in the trash or put it in a collection center..

  • If you are not able to do the above, throw them in garbage bins or containers in your area to make sure they go to a designated place

  • Do not throw it in the street, public places or cultivated areas.

  • Do not throw it in rivers, canals, seas or oceans; Because that would pollute the water and harm marine creatures.

Recycling


  • Recycling PVC is almost impractical due to various additives.

safety:


  • Extremely volatile and one of the least recyclable plastics

  • It causes major problems in the hormonal system.

  • Some other highly toxic chemicals such as DEHA can be produced during its life cycle such as phthalates, lead, dioxins, mercury and cadmium

  • Affects children's growth, immune system and endocrine glands.

  • Causes cancer.

  • Relatively impermeable to sunlight and weather.

  • May cause allergy symptoms in children.
