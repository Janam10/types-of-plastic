Sixth Code

configuration:

  • This plastic is based on polystyrene (PS) or Styrofoam

  • We meet it in the form of rigid polystyrene and formed Styrofoam

How to use:


  • It should be avoided or at least reused

  • It is widely used in packaging and insulation. Therefore, it can be found in drinking glasses, CDs, DVs, egg cartons, ready-to-eat containers, disposable tableware, and insulation including building insulation.

How to get rid:


  • When not recycled, it takes hundreds and hundreds of years to decompose!

  • Since foam products tend to break into smaller pieces, you should put them in a bag, squeeze the air, and tie them up before putting them in the trash to prevent the pellets from spreading

  • Small pieces of it can be found on many beaches and in marine organisms. This plastic has been ingested by countless marine species, which has had incalculable consequences for their health, so do not throw it in rivers, canals, seas or oceans; Because that would pollute the water and harm marine creatures

  • If you are not able to contribute to recycling it, throw it in garbage bins or containers in your area to make sure it goes to a designated place

  • Do not throw it in the street, public places or cultivated areas.

safety:


  • Cheap to produce, light in weight, and easily machined

  • Its problem lies in its fragility, as it disintegrates easily and leaks into the environment

  • Contains styrene that can be filtered during heating

  • Possible human carcinogen

  • Chemicals in polystyrene have been linked to human health and reproductive system impairment

  • Not safe for use in the microwave.
