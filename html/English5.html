Fifth Code

configuration:

  • The plastic marked with No. 5 is Polypropylene (PP)

  • Polypropylene is similar to polyethylene, except that it contains propylene monomers instead of ethylene monomers.

How to use:


  • Found in: Some milk containers, syrup and medicine bottles and lids

  • Can be reused more than once

How to get rid:


    • It takes 20-30 years for this species to decompose, so:
    • If you are not able to contribute to recycling it, throw it in garbage bins or containers in your area to make sure it goes to a designated place

    • Do not throw it in the street, public places or cultivated areas.

    • Do not throw it in rivers, canals, seas or oceans; Because that would pollute the water and harm marine creatures

Recycling


  • It is gradually becoming more acceptable to recyclers.

  • Can be recycled through some sidewalk programs

  • Don't forget to make sure no food is left inside

  • Soft products of this type are best tossed in the trash because they easily slide through screens during recycling and end up as trash anyway

  • Take soft products of this type in a plastic bag to your local drop off center.

safety:


  • It has a high melting point that makes it useful for hot items such as reusable food containers or containers filled with hot food when first filled.

  • It has good chemical resistance to acids, alkalis and most solvents

  • Moisture Resistant

  • Good electrical insulator

  • It holds up to rough handling as you can twist and bend it, and it will keep its shape.

  • It is not resistant to solvents and aromatics - so it can absorb the smell of the substance inside

  • It is considered microwave friendly but it just means that the heated product will not deform in the microwave but some studies prove that even safe plastic that can be placed in the microwave can cause asthma and hormone disturbance, so it is better to replace plastic containers with glass
