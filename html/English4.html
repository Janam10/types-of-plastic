configuration:

  • Plastic 4 or Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE) is a thermoplastic and one of the oldest grades of polyethylene.

  • This type of plastic has the simplest chemical structure of a plastic polymer.

  • Made of petroleum which can be found translucent or opaque.

How to use:

    • It is used in:
    • bread packaging, squeezable bottles, and shopping bags.
    • packaging foam, trays, containers and other plastic wraps.

  • You must reuse it at least a few times before moving it to the trash.

  • It can hold your food products without filtering any harmful substances or allowing microorganisms to permeate
  • It should be used only once and not reused.

  • It should be used only once and not reused.

  • Repeated use increases the growth of bacteria.

  • Never heat it as this causes antimony, a toxic chemical, to leak out.

How to get rid:


  • Taking into account the recycling facilities of this type

  • You will need to check with your local authority to make sure it is recycled in your area

  • Anything made of low-density polyethylene (such as toothpaste tubes) can be thrown in the trash

  • Do not throw it in the street, public places or cultivated areas.

  • Do not throw it in rivers, canals, seas or oceans; Because that would pollute the water and harm marine creatures

Recycling


  • Not environmentally friendly as only a small percentage is recycled

  • Some plants accept it, but their number is very few

  • Plastic shopping bags can often be returned to stores for recycling.

    • Can be recycled into:
    • liners for trash cans and cans, compost bins, and shipping envelopes.
    • boards, lumber, landscaping binders, and floor tiles

safety:


  • Fairly safe to use; as it is safe when in contact with food.

  • It can also cause unhealthy hormonal effects in humans but is safe with food and drink.

  • It is less toxic than other types of plastic.

  • Although there are no known health effects associated with the use of this plastic, organic pollutants form during manufacture
